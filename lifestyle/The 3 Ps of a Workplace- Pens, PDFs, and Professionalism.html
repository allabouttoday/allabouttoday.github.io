The 3 Ps of a Workplace- Pens, PDFs, and Professionalism

There are around 7.85 billion people in the world, out of which 70% are working. Humans have always been in a rat race to be successful in whatever jobs they do.
Even though 70% of the total population has a job right now, why is it that only a few people become successful in what they do? Why is it that we know Bill Gates and Mukesh Ambani, but not the other business magnates that also work?
"Insane Work-Ethic and Hard Work Beat Talent all the time."
Talent is not all you need to make yourself successful; you need to have the professionalism and hard work to back you up all the time. Let's understand the meaning and importance of professionalism in this article.

What does professionalism mean?

Professionalism is such a commonly used word, but it has much technical and profound meaning. In general, it means the knowledge that an individual has and implements in a particular field. Professionalism at work emphasizes a person is doing his/her job with sincerity, integrity, and maintaining professional etiquette at their respective workplace.
This results in logical, unbiased thinking and decision-making, which are the two most important things for the right- work environment. Professionalism is utterly bereft of mind games and politics. It mainly focuses on those who provide quality work with the utmost honesty.
We hear time and again that professionalism at work is essential, but why is it so?

Importance of Professionalism

Professionalism at work is significant because it ensures a fair and sincere performance by all the employees. If everyone is professional at the workplace, they will give 100% effort in the work they do.
Professionalism at the workplace ensures good team spirit, which leads to teamwork. If the employees work professionally, they will understand the values of their personal goals and the organization's goals.
Implementing professionalism and following work ethics at a workplace helps to keep all the employees motivated. Happy employees deliver more and help the company move forward. Professionalism is essential to maintain a good reputation for the company.
In a company where so many employees work, it is vital to recognize and justify everyone's work. Not only quantity but the quality of work matters; practicing professionalism in a company encourages the employees to work harder and deliver quality work.

Final Thoughts

"Professionalism is knowing how to do it, when to do it, and doing it." - Frank Tyger.
It's not just about knowing and understanding this term, but it also has to be implemented at the right time. Professionalism is not something that can be taught; it has to be one's own decision to behave and work professionally.
It has to be maintained all the time, irrespective of the size of the company. It ensures a positive work environment and encourages the employees to give their best all the time.
Sometimes people tend to ignore it and may not consider this important, but it is one of the vital driving forces, which shapes the company and makes it better and better with each passing day.
