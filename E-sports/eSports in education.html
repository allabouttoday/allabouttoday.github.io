E-Sports in Education

In the early 80s, video games came into the market and attracted a vast audience from all age groups. Since then children, teenagers, and adults love playing video games. Esports or electronic sports are the competitive way of playing video games-exploding their popularity with professional esports leagues and tournaments.

According to a market research report published by NewZoo, the audience of Esports raised to 453.8 million worldwide in 2019 with the expected 15% increase of growth each year.

Value of Esports in Education

In the United States, 480 colleges all over the country have included esports in their curriculum, just like athletes, good video gaming players are also getting opportunities to earn scholarships for higher education. The trend is because two-thirds of the 13+ US population are gamers, high video gaming skills are positively impacting the attractiveness in their STEM studies.
Michael Russell, a teacher at Complete High School after attending a convention hosted by Blizzard Entertainment pitched the idea of bringing Esports into his school says “Esports is not just about playing video games” “You can be a storyboard writer or an animator”, You can work on the creativity side or the management side of the gaming industry. This industry is full of opportunities for young minds and a cool thing to bring in schools. He observed that eighty percent of his students participating in Esports had never participated in any extracurricular activity in school before, Now they are actively participating in other activities as well and their GPA improved too.
There are many other stories where Esports is helping educational institutions, teachers in giving their students smart learning experience with creative ways of learning.

Conclusion

Esports in schools is raising the awareness to opt for the smart way of learning among children and teenagers. The education institutions including colleges or schools need to explore and understand its benefits and should come up with certain guidelines for their students and their parents to follow. At an early age, young minds cannot understand the effect of excessive craziness of playing video games, so they need the monitoring and attention of their teachers and parents both.
